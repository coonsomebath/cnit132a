Why keywords matter

Keywords matter because these are the nuggets of thought that contain the most meaning. In reading any sentence, you can omit a lot of the filler words and just capture the nouns and verbs and the message of the sentence can still be resolved. The average person does not want to exert more effort than is necessary and wants to get the most result with the least amount of effort. When conducting a search, a person does not want to type a paragraph to prompt/search for something. They will just type the most important words to convey the meaning and hope the search engine will take care of the rest and read their mind and show exactly what they intended to find.

Search Engine Optimization helps to find these keywords that people tend to use to find things. Optimizing a website for these keywords is like tapping into a bell curve to extract/target individuals that lay somewhere on that curve. You can go for the crowded middle, or you can go for something further out along the curve. The further out on the curve you go, the lower the probability you will find a hit. In other words, the less people you will reach. However, it also means that the probability that those individuals find you increases; it is a give and take. So in SEO optimization, you choose the words or phrases that match your expectation for user acquisition. However, one has to keep in mind that if you go for the crowded middle, the more competition you will face from other "fishermen" on the same pier. While "there are plenty of fishes in the sea", they are harder to catch when you are being "short stopped" by the competition.

There are various places you can place these keywords that will offer a higher return on your effort. You can place them in the html title tags, image tags in the alt and title attributes, in the main content of the webpage, and also in the description in meta tags. I would argue that the description attribute of the meta tags is a very important place. This is because this is the text that shows up on Google's search engine response page. This description shows up just below the hypertext that links to the webpage/website. The user will read this before deciding to expend the effort to click on the link.
